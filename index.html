Case Study: Designing and Launching the AI-Powered Conversational Marketing Unit (Envoy).
V-2.0 After UX
As Product Designer, I led the end-to-end design of the new Envoy conversational marketing unit. In the idealization phase, I defined the product vision, integrating an initial DMO partner request for a display ad unit. Leveraging competitive analysis as my primary research, I informed initial user flows, interactions, and visual language. My design ensured the ad unit seamlessly fit within the fixed max height UI on both desktop and mobile. I then created detailed wireframes and prototypes.

The business sought a new, high-value product: an AI-powered conversational unit to serve as a 'digital ambassador' for Destination Marketing Organizations (DMOs). The core challenge was to conceptualize and design this entirely new product to engage travelers and drive meaningful conversions.
V-2.0 Before UX
My solution was to re-envision the UI layout entirely. I created a much cleaner, intuitive design that seamlessly integrated the suggestion bubbles, optimizing space and refining hierarchy to significantly improve user experience and performance. I designed and prototyped this enhancement, collaborating closely with AI and engineering teams for its successful implementation.
After initial launch, the marketing team requested the addition of suggestion bubbles. Implemented without UX input, these additions led to a crowded design, and subsequent data showed low user interaction and a drop in overall engagement.
Envoy now successfully features numerous DMOs from around the world, showcasing its broad adoption and scalability.

Case Study: Designing High-Impact Media Units for Enhanced User Experience.
To combat "banner blindness," I was tasked with transforming space traditionally used for static banners into useful, native-feeling tools that would enhance the user's media experience and drive performance.
As a UX/UI Product Designer, I led the end-to-end design of various media units. My philosophy was to integrate these units seamlessly by analyzing existing performance data and publisher feedback.
The success of this small change, which more than doubled the initial click-through rate, validated the importance of product design and led to a more robust, research-driven process. My design process was grounded in a blend of user testing, A/B and multivariate testing, and quantitative data analysis. This allowed me to gather qualitative insights from travelers, validate design hypotheses, and track user behavior to ensure every decision was data-driven.
